## Customer Avatars (from Creative Bible)

Use one avatar per ad. Match the hook, script formula, and CTA to the avatar's awareness stage.

### 1. The Plateaued Professional
**Who:** Mid-level manager, 28-38. Promoted for technical skills, now in communication-heavy role.
**Core problem:** Gets told to "work on executive presence" with no path forward.
**Emotional state:** Frustrated, quietly insecure. Doubts whether more content helps.
**What they say:** "I've read every communication book and still freeze in meetings." / "I was passed over for someone who presents better."
**Best angles:** Problem, Identity, Comparison
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware (Level 2)

### 2. The Fluent But Invisible
**Who:** Non-native English speaker, 25-40, corporate environment. Vocabulary is fine.
**Core problem:** Technically fluent but sounds less authoritative than native-speaking peers with less expertise.
**Emotional state:** Isolated, overlooked, growing resentful.
**What they say:** "I can order coffee in London but I don't sound like a leader in the boardroom." / "My accent isn't the problem — something else is off."
**Best angles:** Problem, Mechanism, Identity
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware (Level 2)

### 3. The Content Creator Stuck on Delivery
**Who:** YouTube/TikTok/podcast creator, 22-35. Great ideas, weak delivery.
**Core problem:** Content quality is high but engagement is low. Knows the problem is delivery.
**Emotional state:** Self-critical, envious of creators who "just sound natural."
**What they say:** "I sound boring even when the content is good." / "How do some people just have 'it'?"
**Best angles:** Problem, Mechanism, Education
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware (Level 3)

### 4. The Interview Prep Sprinter
**Who:** Job seeker or career switcher, 24-40. 2-4 weeks before a critical interview.
**Core problem:** Knows speaking lets them down in high-stakes moments. Time-pressured.
**Emotional state:** Anxious, urgent, willing to pay for a quick fix.
**What they say:** "I have a big interview next week and I freeze every time." / "I lost the job to someone less qualified who interviewed better."
**Best angles:** Problem, Urgency, Comparison
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware (Level 3)

### 5. The Toastmasters Dropout
**Who:** Professional, 30-50. Tried Toastmasters or public speaking workshops.
**Core problem:** Believes in training but couldn't stick with existing solutions — too slow, too generic.
**Emotional state:** Discouraged but not defeated. Wants self-paced, private, technology-driven.
**What they say:** "Toastmasters was too slow — I need daily practice, not biweekly speeches." / "I don't want to practice in front of strangers."
**Best angles:** Comparison, Mechanism, Education
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware (Level 3)

### 6. The HR/L&D Budget Holder
**Who:** HR manager or L&D lead, 32-50. Responsible for team development.
**Core problem:** Budget for communication training but every platform delivers content employees ignore. Can't prove ROI.
**Emotional state:** Skeptical of new tools. Burned by shelfware.
**What they say:** "We bought LinkedIn Learning and adoption was 15%." / "How do I prove our managers got better?"
**Best angles:** Comparison, Social Proof, Education
**Awareness level:** Solution/Product Aware (Level 3-4)

### 7. The Founder Who Can't Pitch
**Who:** Startup founder or small business owner, 26-45.
**Core problem:** Loses deals and investor meetings — not because of the idea but because of delivery. Can't afford a $200/hour coach.
**Emotional state:** Frustrated, self-aware, high urgency.
**What they say:** "My product sells itself when people try it. The problem is getting them to try it." / "I watch my pitch recordings and I sound nothing like I do in my head."
**Best angles:** Problem, Story, Urgency
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware (Level 2)

### 8. The Post-Language-App Graduate
**Who:** Non-native speaker, 20-35. Used Praktika, Duolingo, or Speak to reach fluency.
**Core problem:** Gap between "functional English" and "impressive English" — vocal, not linguistic.
**Emotional state:** Ready for the next step but unsure where to go.
**What they say:** "Praktika got me to fluent but I still don't sound professional." / "I passed IELTS. Why don't people take me seriously in meetings?"
**Best angles:** Problem, Mechanism, Identity
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware (Level 2)

### 9. The Microlearning Refugee
**Who:** Self-improvement app user, 25-40. Cycled through Headway, Blinkist, SmartyMe.
**Core problem:** Consumed hundreds of summaries about communication but nothing changed. The knowing-doing gap.
**Emotional state:** Cynical about self-improvement apps. Ready to be surprised by something genuinely different.
**What they say:** "I've read 200 book summaries about communication and still sound the same." / "These apps make you feel productive without actually being productive."
**Best angles:** Comparison, Mechanism, Problem
**Awareness level:** Solution/Product Aware (Level 3-4)

### 10. The Anxious Speaker
**Who:** Professional at any level, 22-55. Freezes in meetings, avoids presentations.
**Core problem:** Not a knowledge problem — a physical/emotional response to speaking situations.
**Emotional state:** Ashamed, avoidant, quietly desperate. Has organized career around avoiding speaking.
**What they say:** "My voice shakes when all eyes are on me." / "I've turned down promotions because they'd require presenting." / "I feel physically sick before team meetings."
**Best angles:** Problem, Story, Education
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware (Level 2)
