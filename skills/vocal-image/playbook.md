## Product

**Vocal Image** is an AI-powered speaking coach app.
Daily voice practice with real-time AI feedback on pace, clarity, filler words, vocal confidence, and delivery.
**Price:** ~$9.99/month (~$120/year)
**Website:** vocalimage.app
**Category:** AI communication coaching / vocal training / speaking skills

**Core differentiator:** Practice-based, not content-based. The only app that trains HOW YOU SOUND through daily vocal exercises with AI feedback — not lessons about communication, but actual speaking practice that changes your voice.

**The physical mechanism:** The voice works through resonance and breath release. A tight, forced tone signals stress. A free, connected voice signals authority. Vocal Image teaches release — not control.

**Target:** Professionals, non-native speakers, founders, content creators — anyone whose career depends on how they communicate and who has consumed advice about speaking but never actually practiced speaking.

## Ad Structure

Every ad follows one of 8 script formulas from the Creative Bible. The most common:

### SF1: PAS — Problem → Agitate → Solve
Best for: Problem Aware audience, Sophistication Level 2-3
- Hook (3-5s): Question or call-out matching avatar's awareness
- Problem (5-10s): State the problem in exact customer language
- Agitate (5-15s): Hidden costs, emotional toll, compounding frustration
- Solve (5-10s): Introduce product through its mechanism (practice, not content)
- Proof (5-10s): One specific proof point
- CTA (3-5s): One direct action

### SF2: BAB — Before → After → Bridge
Best for: Problem/Solution Aware, Sophistication Level 2-3
- Hook: Story or call-out
- Before: Paint current reality viscerally
- After: Specific transformation (not "easier" — exactly what changes)
- Bridge: Product as the path between states
- CTA: Direct or soft depending on awareness

### SF3: AIDA — Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
Best for: Unaware/Problem Aware, cold audiences, Sophistication Level 1-2
- Attention: Pattern interrupt or statistic hook
- Interest: Educate on the problem
- Desire: Stack benefit + proof + social proof
- Action: Single CTA (soft for low awareness, direct for higher)

### SF4: Story Arc — Setup → Conflict → Resolution
Best for: Solution/Product Aware, Sophistication Level 4-5
- Open with a real situation (no generic "Hi I'm...")
- Conflict with exact customer frustration language
- Discovery: How they found the product (not "I saw an ad")
- Resolution: Specific, concrete transformation
- CTA: Soft — the story sells

## Hook Patterns (from real Vocal Image podcast scripts)

The proven hook format: **"Why do/don't [specific situation happen]?"**

Real hooks that work:
- "Why don't your colleagues listen when you speak in meetings?"
- "Why does your voice shake when you're nervous?"
- "The biggest game changer for leadership confidence is your voice."
- "Why don't your presentations land the way you want?"
- "The secret to a powerful first impression isn't your handshake — it's your voice."
- "Why do some salespeople close deals while others don't?"
- "Why does your voice fade on Zoom?"
- "Is it possible to sound more confident in English in just days?"

Hook rules:
- Max 1 sentence
- Names a specific situation, not a generic problem
- No intros, no "Hi I'm..." openings
- Repeat the hook at the end (wrap structure)

## The Real Mechanism (never oversimplify this)

Vocal Image's point of difference is the **physical voice mechanism**, not generic "confidence tips":

- Voice issues come from tension in the body leaking into the voice
- The fix is **release**, not control: relax breath, connect vocal cords, let voice resonate
- "Authority isn't loudness; it's a steady, connected vibration"
- "It's not about controlling breath — it's about releasing it with trust"
- "Real authority comes from a calm, confident tone — not a shout"

This mechanism must be present in every script — either explicitly or implied.

- Goal: drive to vocalimage.app

### App Installs
- Goal: direct install on App Store / Play Store
- Tone: fast, direct, benefit-first
- Length: 15–30s

## Production Pipeline (actual workflow)

1. **Script** — Creative Bible → pick avatar + awareness + script formula → write
2. **Video generation** — Higgsfield (reference image control for Aria/James/Nick)
   - UUID filenames from Higgsfield go in `higgs/` folder
   - Freepik for B-roll footage (realistic body clips, office shots)
   - Screen recordings of the Vocal Image app
3. **Editing** — DaVinci Resolve (primary tool for all final output)
   - App mockups: Dynamic Island UI Pack + MOCKUP files from ASSETS/
   - Subtitles added in Resolve (word-level)
   - CTA sequences rendered separately, composited
4. **Audio** — ElevenLabs for narration; custom SFX from CREATIVE ADS/sfx/
5. **Export** — H264, 1080×1920, 30fps, AAC audio

### Naming convention
`S[number]_V[version_number]` — e.g. S267_V3.mov
Reworks: `S[number]_RW[n]`
Language variants: prefix with `ESP_`, `FR_`, `DE_`

## Visual Rules

1. **9:16 vertical** is default. Always.
2. Characters shot against clean backgrounds — no clutter
3. App screen recordings are a core visual element (shows the product working)
4. Character consistency: always use reference sheets — never generate without reference
5. Subtitles in every final export
6. CTA cards rendered as separate elements and composited

## What Makes a Winner (from real Vocal Image content)

- Hook names a specific, relatable situation in the first 2 seconds
- The physical mechanism is mentioned (release/resonance), not just "speak better"
- Avatar match is tight — one specific person, not everyone
- CTA matches the campaign type (install vs. visit)
- Clean audio — Aria/Nick voices are calm, resonant, not rushed
- App screen shown (proof the product exists and works)

## What Makes a Loser

- Opens with product name or brand intro
- Hook is generic ("Communication is important")
- Multiple avatars targeted in one ad
- Mechanism missing — just says "get confident"
- CTA asks too much or has two actions
- Mehrabian myth: never use "93% of communication is nonverbal" — scientifically false and industry-known as a myth
- Made-up statistics — tag all unverified numbers as {VERIFY}
